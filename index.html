Quick primer — what is a prediction market?

A prediction market is a marketplace where participants buy and sell shares tied to the outcome of a specific, verifiable event. Each share’s **price** can be read as the market’s probability that the event happens. For example, a share priced at $0.42 implies a 42% market probability.

How price ↔ probability works
• Price is expressed between $0.00 and $1.00 per share. $1.00 pays out $1 if the event occurs, $0 otherwise.
• Price ≈ probability. Example: $0.75 → market expects ~75% chance. Prices move as traders buy/sell based on new information.

Operational flow — how to participate

1. Sign up — Create an account and complete KYC where required (we launch under Dubai / Singapore regs).
2. Deposit funds — Add fiat or supported stablecoins. Balances appear in your wallet on the platform.
3. Browse markets — Each market lists an event, resolution criteria, and current price.
4. Buy / Sell shares — Market or limit orders. Buying pushes price up, selling pushes it down.
5. Resolution — When the event resolves (using public, verifiable sources), winning shares payout $1 per share; losing shares are worth $0.

Example transactions (walkthroughs)

Example 1 — Simple directional bet
Market: "Will Company X release product Y by June 30, 2026?"
Current price: $0.30 (30% chance). You buy 100 shares at $0.30 → cost $30. If the product launches you receive 100 × $1 = $100; profit = $70 (minus fees). If not, you lose $30.
Example 2 — Trading price changes
You buy 200 shares at $0.45 costing $90. Later, new data moves market to $0.60. You sell 200 shares at $0.60 → receive $120, net profit $30. You realized a trade P&L without waiting for resolution.
Example 3 — Scalping/hedge
You think the market is overvalued at $0.80. You short (sell) 50 shares. If the price drops to $0.50 you buy to close, pocketing the difference. Institutions also use markets to hedge event risk for exposure management.

Glossary (short)

Share: unit representing claim on event outcome.
Price: cost per share (0–1); approximates probability.
Resolution: final verification step determining payout.
Liquidity: ease of buying/selling without big price moves.
